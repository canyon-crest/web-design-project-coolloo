Julian Meng

The Cool Person

Background

On January 11th, 2010, I breathed my first breath. I was born to Chinese immigrants in the city of Vancouver, where I would live for the first three years of my life. I was a rowdy child, often revolting against my parents, resulting in many physical scars for me and mental scars for my parents. After my sister was born, we moved to San Diego, where I attended preschool. I would quietly progress through the rest of my school days, making many new friends along the way.

High School Experience

For the majority of my life, academics have been my strong suit. I breezed through elementary and middle school, achieving A’s in every class. However, when I reached high school, I needed to switch my mindset. The difficulty of high school courses was much higher than middle school classes, and I needed to teach myself study methods to avoid falling behind. I realized the importance of connections, often studying with peers before large assignments and tests. As I received my first B in a class, I learned not to stress myself over mistakes I had already committed, but to focus on avoiding the same mistakes again.

Goals and Aspirations

Ever since I was a small child, my parents have taught me the importance of continuing my education in college. As I have grown older, I have continued this thought, as college is one of my most important lifetime goals. College will not be the end of my ambitions, as I have vague ideas on careers I want to pursue, based on my strengths in high school. However, the most important goal for my entire life is to achieve true happiness. The way to achieve this is by experiencing as many things as possible, including college, as I have stated before, exploring the world, or making new friends.
