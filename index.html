Julian Meng

The Cool Person

Background

I was born on January 11th, 2010, to Chinese immigrants in Vancouver, My early years were adventurous. I often ignored my parents' rules, which led to some bruises. When my sister was born, we moved to San Diego for the warmer weather. I started preschool, where I enjoyed learning and making friends. As I grew up, I faced new challenges and made new friendships. Opening my report cards and seeing my parents proud at school events are memories I treasure. Each experience, like learning to ride a bike, shaped my childhood and gave me unforgettable memories.

High School Experience

Throughout my life, academics have been my strong suit, as I easily made my way through elementary and middle school, earning A’s in every class. However, when I reached high school, I quickly realized that I needed to change my mindset to keep up with the tougher coursework. This shift pushed me to figure out effective study methods to stay on track. I also learned how important it was to connect with my peers, often working together to prepare for big assignments and tests, which helped me understand the material better. When I got my first B in a class, it taught me a valuable lesson: instead of stressing over past mistakes, I should focus on learning from them and making sure not to repeat them in the future. This mindset not only reduced my stress but also helped me grow as a student.

Fun Fact

  • My dream university is UC Berkeley!

Goals and Aspirations

Since I was a young child, my parents have taught me the importance of continuing my education, especially in college. As I’ve grown older, this belief has only become stronger, making college one of my main goals in life. I know that college will not be the end of my dreams, as I have some ideas about potential careers that match my strengths from high school. However, the most important goal for me is to find true happiness. I believe that I can achieve this by trying out as many experiences as possible, whether that is going to college, exploring the world, or making new friends along the way.
